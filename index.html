Eligiblity

WHO CAN GIVE BLOOD

    • Age- between 18 and 60 years

    • Haemoglobin - not less than 12.5 g/Dl

    • Pulse - between 50 and 100/minute with no irregularities

    • Blood Pressure -Systolic 100-180 mm Hg and Diastolic 50 - 100 mm Hg

    • Temperature - Normal (oral temperature not exceeding 37.50C)

    • Weight: at least 50 kg.

    (In some countries, donors of whole blood donations should weigh at least 45 kg to donate 350 ml ± 10%.)

YOU CANNOT DONATE BLOOD IF:

    • Chronic illnesses (e.g., diabetes, hypertension)

    • Recent illness or surgery

    • Alcohol and drug use

    • Infections: Cannot donate with a cold, flu, sore throat, cold sore, stomach bug, Screening
    for HIV, Hepatitis B and C, and other blood-borne infections

    • Tattoos/Piercings: Wait 6 months after a tattoo or body piercing, or 12 hours if performed
    by a registered health professional and fully healed.

    • Dental Work: Wait 24 hours after minor procedures and 1 month after major dental work.

    • During Pregnancy: Pregnant women cannot donate blood due to their increased blood
    needs.

    • Postpartum Period: Women should wait 6 months to 1 year after giving birth before donating
    blood to allow for recovery.

    • Breastfeeding Mothers: Typically, wait at least 6 months after childbirth and ensure
    breastfeeding is well-established before donating.

    • Nutritional Considerations: Ensure iron levels are sufficient to support both mother and baby.

General Advice

    • Consult Healthcare Providers: Speak with a healthcare provider before donating.

    • Follow Local Guidelines: Check with local blood donation centers for specific rules
